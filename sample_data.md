### 1. Finding Peace in the Storm
**Category:** peace  
**Tags:** peace, psalms, comfort, storms  
**Excerpt:** When life's storms rage around us, where do we find our anchor? Discover how Psalm 46 became my lighthouse in the darkest nights.  
**Content:**
```
Life has a way of throwing storms at us when we least expect them. The phone call that changes everything. The diagnosis that stops time. The loss that leaves us gasping for air.

I remember sitting in that hospital waiting room, watching the rain streak down the windows, feeling like the storm outside was nothing compared to the tempest in my heart. It was then that the words of Psalm 46:10 whispered to my soul: "Be still, and know that I am God."

In that moment of forced stillness, I discovered something profound. Peace isn't the absence of storms—it's the presence of God in the midst of them. The psalmist knew this truth when he wrote, "God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble."

When we can't see the shore, when the waves seem too high, when our strength fails—that's when we discover that our God is bigger than any storm we face. He doesn't always calm the storm, but He always calms His child.

Today, whatever storm you're facing, remember: you are not alone in the boat. The One who speaks to winds and waves is right there with you, whispering peace to your troubled heart.

*Selah* - pause and reflect on His faithfulness in your storms.
```

### 2. Grace in the Grieving
**Category:** gratitude  
**Tags:** grief, healing, grace, loss  
**Excerpt:** Grief is not the enemy of faith—it's the birthplace of deeper grace. A journey through loss that led to unexpected gifts.  
**Content:**
```
They tell you that grief comes in stages, as if it's a neat, orderly process you can check off a list. But grief isn't linear—it's a spiral, sometimes taking you deeper, sometimes lifting you higher, always changing you.

When I lost my beloved husband, I thought I had lost everything. The future we had planned, the dreams we had shared, the simple joy of morning coffee together—all gone in an instant. I raged at God, questioned His goodness, and wondered if faith was just a beautiful lie we tell ourselves.

But in the depths of that valley, something unexpected happened. Grace met me there.

Not the kind of grace that takes away the pain—that would come later, in small doses. But the grace that sits with you in the darkness, that holds you when you can't hold yourself, that whispers "I know" when words fail.

I learned that grief is not the opposite of gratitude—it's gratitude with tears. Every pang of loss is actually love with nowhere to go, and love is always something to be grateful for.

The Psalms became my prayer book during those dark months. David knew about loss, about crying out to God from the depths. In Psalm 30:5, he reminds us that "weeping may endure for a night, but joy comes in the morning."

My morning didn't come quickly, but it did come. And when it did, I discovered that grief had not destroyed my capacity for joy—it had deepened it.

*Selah* - let your tears water the seeds of tomorrow's gratitude.
```

### 3. The Sacred Pause
**Category:** worship  
**Tags:** selah, reflection, worship, presence  
**Excerpt:** In a world that never stops, the ancient word "Selah" teaches us the sacred art of the pause. Discovering God in the spaces between.  
**Content:**
```
Selah. It appears 71 times in the Psalms, this mysterious word that scholars debate and hearts understand. Some say it means "pause," others "lift up," still others "forever." I think it means all of these and more.

In our breathless world of notifications and deadlines, of rushing from one thing to the next, Selah is a revolutionary act. It's permission to stop. To breathe. To be present with the Divine in this moment.

I discovered the power of Selah during my own season of rushing—rushing through grief, rushing toward healing, rushing to feel normal again. But healing, I learned, happens in the pauses, not in the motion.

When the psalmist writes, "The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want" and then adds "Selah," he's not just making a statement—he's creating space for that truth to sink in. He's saying, "Stop here. Let this settle in your soul. Don't rush past this revelation."

In my poetry, in my prayers, in my daily life, I've learned to build in these sacred pauses. Moments where I stop trying to figure everything out and simply rest in what I know to be true: God is good, God is present, God is enough.

The next time you read a psalm and encounter that word Selah, don't skip over it. Stop. Breathe. Let the truth that came before it wash over you like a gentle rain on thirsty ground.

*Selah* - because some truths are too beautiful to rush past.
```

## Volumes (Poetry Collection)

### 1. When Darkness Whispers
**Category:** contemplation  
**Price:** Free  
**Description:** A raw, honest exploration of walking through the valley of the shadow of death and discovering that even there, we are not alone.  
**Content:**
```
When darkness whispers lies about tomorrow,
When shadows dance where light once lived,
When hope feels like a foreign language
I've somehow forgotten how to speak—

That's when I remember:
The valley has a Shepherd too.

His rod and staff, they comfort me
Not because they remove the valley
But because they remind me
I don't walk it alone.

Even here, especially here,
In the place where tears taste like prayers
And silence sounds like worship,
His goodness follows me.

*Selah*

The table He prepares
Isn't always in green pastures—
Sometimes it's set right here
In the presence of my enemies:
Fear, doubt, despair.

But the feast is no less real,
The oil no less healing,
The cup no less overflowing
Because of where we dine.

Surely goodness and mercy
Will follow me all the days of my life—
Even the dark ones,
Especially the dark ones.

*Selah*
```

### 2. Morning Mercies
**Category:** thanksgiving  
**Price:** Free  
**Description:** Celebrating the daily gift of God's renewed mercies, finding gratitude in the ordinary moments that make up an extraordinary life.  
**Content:**
```
They are new every morning,
These mercies that meet me
Before my feet touch the floor,
Before my mind remembers yesterday's worries.

Fresh as the dew on grass,
Gentle as the first light
Painting the sky in watercolors
Of hope and possibility.

I used to think mercy
Was only for the big failures,
The dramatic falls from grace,
The moments that make headlines.

But mercy, I've learned,
Is also in the morning coffee,
The bird song outside my window,
The breath that fills my lungs without asking.

*Selah*

Great is Your faithfulness,
Not just in the grand gestures
But in the thousand small kindnesses
That make up an ordinary day.

The way the sun rises
Without my permission or help,
The way my heart keeps beating
Even when I forget to be grateful.

This is mercy too:
The gift of another beginning,
Another chance to see You
In the sacred ordinary.

*Selah*
```

### 3. Unshakeable
**Category:** faith  
**Price:** Free  
**Description:** An anthem of faith for those whose foundations have been tested, discovering what remains when everything else falls away.  
**Content:**
```
They said the ground beneath me
Was solid, sure, unmoving.
They said if I built my life here
It would stand through any storm.

But earthquakes don't ask permission,
And foundations can crumble
Even when they're made
Of all the right materials.

Career, health, relationships—
All the things I thought were bedrock
Turned out to be shifting sand
When the shaking started.

*Selah*

But in the rubble of my certainties,
I found something unshakeable:
Not a thing or a plan or a person,
But a Presence.

The One who was there
Before the foundation of the world,
Who remains when everything else
Has been shaken loose.

My hope is built on nothing less
Than this unshakeable love,
This unchanging faithfulness,
This God who is the same yesterday, today, forever.

Let the earth give way,
Let the mountains fall into the sea—
I will not fear,
For You are my unshakeable foundation.

*Selah*
```
